Detection

How to measure if a voice is in tune

The first step to pitch correction is detecting the fundamental frequency of a window. First the data is broken up into windows, smaller packets of the signal. In breaking up the data, several techniques may be used, but a prominent one is the hamming window. This attenuates the ends of each window, such that frequencies introduced by cutting a window off from its neighbor are reduced. Next, the fundamental frequency of these windows is found. For example, one window may exhibit several frequencies, but 5 kHz is the dominant frequency. There are several ways to find the fundamental frequency of a window, many use the Fourier Transform.

The Fourier Transform takes some signal in the time domain, where each point in time is mapped to the strength of the signal at that time, and converts it to the frequency domain. In the frequency domain, each frequency in a signal is mapped to the strength of that frequency in the signal. Knowing the strength of a frequency allows the most important pitch in a signal to be determined. Here, DFT2 is used. With DFT2, the Fourier Transform is applied to the Fourier Transform of the signal. The largest peak in the resulting signal is taken as the fundamental frequency.
